Who Am I?

I am a graduate student at the University of Arizona who is majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering. I am very passionate about technology and find myself watching hours of videos on new developments. I plan on being a life long learner as I believe education is imperative and I have a true passion for the classroom setting and collaborative learning. I have research interests in Quantum Mechanics and Quantum Information theory as well as Embedded Systems Security.

Goals

My goal is to be an all around player. I hope to develop my skills in a multitude of areas, from full stack development and software engineering, to embedded systems security and network security. I aspire to find opportunities that prioritize a diverse collaborative work environment.

Senior Design

Elbit Systems of America, tasked my team and I with designing and developing a small, portable infrared beacon (STrOBe) that must be versatile enough to be deployed by drone or hand in rugged environments. Short-wave infrared (SWIR) beacons have been used by the military for marking targets to be destroyed, landing zones for a quick pick-up, and illuminating allied troops to prevent friendly fire. Our team designed a device that can mark a target in the field by emitting at 1550 nm, detectable to those equipped with SWIR vision technology. The STrOBe beacon is small, portable, remotely programmable, rugged, and bright enough to be seen from a distance. The device is controlled remotely via bluetooth connectivity through utilizationg of a phone terminal that allows for turning the beacon on and off, as well as changing the pulse repetition rate. Up to three individual STrOBe devices can be controlled using this same app.Personally, I was responsible for not only developing the firmware, but also the hardware to support the functionality of our design. The hardware design was done through utilizing tools such as OrCad Spice, Eagle PCB, as well as hand drawn sketches that required me to meet the constraints set in place by Elbit.
